Andrew I. Christianson

Founder

Andrew is an internet-native founder obsessed with compounding: products that keep getting better, audiences that keep growing, and ventures that turn into real, durable businesses. He splits his time between distribution, product, and making sure Operario AI is investing in the right things in the right order. Before Operario AI, he bootstrapped and shipped multiple SaaS products, grew open-source projects 9× in 90 days, and spent a decade building dataflow and mission-critical infrastructure for the U.S. National Security Agency, where failure wasn't an option.

  • Founder of Operario AI AI, a platform for browser-native AI agents that take over the repetitive online work so human teams can focus on strategy and relationships.
  • Creator of RA.Aid, the open-source coding agent selected as the inaugural project for Open Core Ventures' Catalyst program.
  • Multi-time founder, from bootstrapped SaaS to AI-native consumer products, with a bias toward compounding over one-off wins.
  • Former NSA contractor and long-time contributor to Apache NiFi/MiNiFi, the open-source dataflow project originally released by the U.S. National Security Agency; that work underpinned Onyara (acquired by Hortonworks) and Datavolo (acquired by Snowflake).
  • Public presence as "AI Christianson", where 60k+ people follow his work on AI, infrastructure, and company-building as a live feedback loop with the market.
